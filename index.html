I am currently a Research Professor at Chosun Babylab and the Center for Data Science in the Humanities at Chosun University in Gwangju, South Korea.

My research focuses on early language development, especially how young children learn from everyday linguistic input. Using methods such as eye-tracking, naturalistic recordings, corpus analysis, and computational modelling, I investigate language acquisition across both experimental and real-world settings.

During my PhD, I developed Bayesian IRT-based adaptive assessments for tracking early vocabulary development in multilingual children. These brief, browser-based tools were designed to support efficient assessment and targeted intervention, and have been integrated into e-Babylab.

My recent work examines Korean child-directed speech, caregiver-child interaction, and computational approaches to word segmentation and language assessment. I have also worked as a Lecturer at Sunway University, where I taught large undergraduate courses and developed skills in experimental design, quantitative analysis, assessment design, and research supervision.

I am also experimenting with a tool for testing emotion perception.

During my free time, I enjoy swimming, playing tennis, and reading.
