I am currently a post-doctoral researcher at the Chosun Babylab at the Chosun University in Gwangju, Korea

My research focuses on unraveling the learning mechanisms of early language development in young children. By using multiple methods like eye-tracking, naturalistic recordings, and corpus analysis, I investigate early language learning comprehensively to examine the factors shaping early language acquisition.

To better track language development, I developed innovative, adaptive assessments using Bayesian IRT models during my PhD. These brief, browser-based tests provide insights into children's linguistic abilities for targeted interventions. This tool is intergrated in e-Babylab .

I am currently toying with a tool for testing emotion perception.
